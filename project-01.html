Design Generator

Geometry
as Acoustics

The construction lines share the same angular ratios as the acoustic propagation angles of the Goldener Saal in Vienna — one of the world's most precisely tuned concert halls.

The same geometric ratio governing optical behavior in the object governs acoustic behavior in the hall. From this correspondence, the two auditoriums were sized and oriented.

Structure
Made Visible

The section reveals the full depth of the building system — photovoltaic glass roof panels sit above the primary steel truss, which descends through the auditorium to the concrete slab below.

The building's interior is a performance of structure: exposed trusses, layered glazing, and shifting floor plates create a space that is simultaneously infrastructure and stage.

Construction Details — Chunk Selection

Three Chunks,
Three Systems

Three construction chunks were selected to resolve the building's most complex intersections: the roof edge, the mesh facade, the glass canopy, and the auditorium seating wall.

Façade

A City
Reflected

The façade responds directly to the dense Viennese urban fabric surrounding the site. Existing building elevations were studied and abstracted into the cladding system.

The building is simultaneously a new object within the city and a surface that reflects it back — a mirror-mask that belongs to its context by distorting it.

Circulation Strategy

Movement as
Performance

The exit strategy is layered across three floors. Double elevators connect to the first floor; the auditorium seating cascades toward the stage in a continuous raked section that doubles as escape route.

Model

A process of construction and reflection. Translating architectural ideas into material performance. The model captures the project's central idea: a fractured container where performance, structure, and circulation collide.
