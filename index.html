04

Lymphtonic

Downtown Los Angeles — 2024

A self-healing building powered by algae that adapts to its environment. Fitness and data infrastructure share the same metabolic logic: both generate heat, require controlled atmosphere, and depend on continuous circulatory flow. The section forces them into a single structural frame where their shared biology becomes the architecture.

Future Ruins

2B Visual Studies — Sao Paulo

A short film depicting a hypothetical post-apocalyptic environment focused on the MASP museum in Sao Paulo. The ruin is not accidental but designed every element of decay was choreographed to reveal how the building would fail. The structural skeleton remains partially intact, reclaimed by overgrowth.

About
SCI-Arc · Los Angeles · Est. 2022

Emanuele Di Paola is an architecture student at SCI-Arc in Los Angeles, pursuing a BArch degree (2022–2027). His work spans cultural centers, institutional buildings, speculative structures, and short films always exploring the relationship between structure, movement, and spatial experience.

He brings a multidisciplinary approach combining computational design, physical model-making, and digital fabrication. His practice bridges rigorous technical development with conceptual depth.
